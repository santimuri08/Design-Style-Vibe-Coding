## üéØ What Is Editorial Swiss?

Editorial Swiss is the application of Swiss Design principles to long-form content and editorial layouts. It emphasizes typographic hierarchy, readability, structured information, and elegant treatment of text-heavy pages. Think of it as Swiss Design optimized for reading experiences‚Äîarticles, blogs, documentation, journals.

**Core Philosophy:**
- Typography is the interface
- Hierarchy through scale and spacing
- Grid structures long-form content
- Readability and clarity paramount
- Elegant restraint (not decorative)
- Whitespace frames content
- Systematic approach to editorial design

**Not to be confused with:** Magazine design (more decorative) or Swiss Punk (kinetic, rebellious)

## üé® Key Visual Characteristics

### Typography (The Core)

**Typeface Choices:**
- **Body text:** Serif preferred (Charter, Freight Text, Georgia, Garamond)
- **Headings:** Sans-serif (Helvetica, Suisse Int'l, Inter, Akzidenz-Grotesk)
- **Contrast:** Serif body + sans headings creates hierarchy
- **Alternative:** All sans-serif (Univers, Frutiger) for different feel

**Why serif for body?**
More readable in long-form content (traditional editorial choice).

**Type Scale (Large, Clear):**
```
H1: 48-56px (article title, dramatic)
H2: 36-42px (major sections)
H3: 28-32px (subsections)
H4: 24px (minor headings)
Body: 18-21px (generous, readable)
Small: 16px minimum
Captions: 14-16px, muted color
```

**Key Principles:**
- **Large body text:** 18-21px (not 16px)
- **Dramatic headings:** h1 significantly larger
- **Clear hierarchy:** 6-8pt jumps between levels
- **Limited weights:** Regular + Bold sufficient
- **Italics for emphasis:** Not bold in body text

**Line Height & Measure:**
```
Body text: 1.7-1.8 line-height (generous)
Headings: 1.2-1.3 line-height (tighter)
Measure: 65-75 characters per line (optimal)
Max-width: 680-720px for text blocks
```

### Visual Hierarchy (Essential)

**Hierarchy Techniques:**
1. **Scale:** Dramatically different sizes (h1 vs. body)
2. **Weight:** Bold for headings, regular for body
3. **Spacing:** Generous space before headings
4. **Color:** Muted for secondary text
5. **Typeface:** Serif vs. sans contrast

**Example hierarchy:**
- Article title (h1): 56px, bold, sans-serif, black
- Section heading (h2): 36px, bold, sans-serif, black
- Subsection (h3): 28px, bold, sans-serif, black
- Body: 21px, regular, serif, dark gray (#333)
- Captions: 16px, regular, sans-serif, medium gray (#666)
- Metadata: 14px, regular, sans-serif, light gray (#999)

### Layout & Grid

**Single-Column Preferred:**
For long-form reading, single column is optimal.

**Grid Structure:**
```
Main content column: 680-720px width
Side margins: Generous (15-20% of viewport)
Vertical rhythm: 24-32px spacing between elements
Max-width container: 1200px (for centered layout)
```

**Multi-column for specific content:**
- Pull quotes (offset from main text)
- Sidenotes or margin notes
- Image captions alongside images
- Related content sidebars

**Asymmetric layouts:**
8-4 or 7-5 column splits (main content + sidebar)

### Color Palette
- **Minimal color:** Black, grays, white
- **Text:** #000 or #111 for headings, #333 for body
- **Secondary text:** #666 (captions), #999 (metadata)
- **Background:** White (#FFF) or off-white (#FAFAFA)
- **Accent:** Optional single color for links (#0066CC)
- **Dividers:** Light gray (#E0E0E0)

**No decoration:**
Color serves hierarchy and function, not decoration.

### Visual Elements

**Images:**
- Full-width or contained within text column
- Generous spacing around images (48-64px)
- Captions directly below (16px, gray, sans-serif)
- High-quality photography or illustrations
- Avoid stock photos that feel generic

**Quotes:**
- Pull quotes: 28-36px, serif, centered or offset
- Blockquotes: Indented, italic, gray
- Attribution: Small, sans-serif, below quote

**Lists:**
- Generous spacing (16-24px between items)
- Simple bullets or numbers
- Hanging indent for clean alignment

**Dividers:**
- 1px horizontal rules (light gray)
- Generous spacing before/after (48px+)
- Use sparingly (white space often better)

### Spacing & Rhythm

**Vertical rhythm crucial:**
```
Paragraph spacing: 24-32px
Before h2: 64-72px
Before h3: 48-56px
Before h4: 32-40px
Image spacing: 48-64px top/bottom
Section spacing: 80-96px
```

**Consistent baseline grid:**
All spacing multiples of 8px (8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 80)

## ‚ùå Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Typography Mistakes
- **Too small:** 16px or smaller body text (use 18-21px)
- **Tight line-height:** Below 1.6 (use 1.7-1.8 for long-form)
- **Long measure:** Over 75 characters per line (hard to read)
- **Weak hierarchy:** Similar sizes for h1-h3
- **All bold body text:** Use italics for emphasis instead
- **Decorative fonts:** Stick to classic serif/sans pairings

### Layout Mistakes
- **Too wide:** Text spanning full viewport (use max-width 680-720px)
- **No margins:** Content touching edges (use generous side margins)
- **Multi-column overuse:** Forcing columns where single is better
- **Inconsistent spacing:** Random vertical rhythm
- **Cluttered:** Not enough white space

### Hierarchy Mistakes
- **Timid differences:** h1 only slightly larger than h2
- **Too many levels:** More than h1-h4 creates confusion
- **Inconsistent treatment:** h2 styled differently in different sections
- **No visual breaks:** Long text with no subheadings

### Color Mistakes
- **Too colorful:** Using multiple accent colors
- **Low contrast:** Light gray text on white
- **Decorative color:** Color for decoration, not function

### Editorial Mistakes
- **No structure:** Missing subheadings and breaks
- **Walls of text:** Long paragraphs without breaks
- **Missing captions:** Images without context
- **Poor metadata:** Author, date, reading time unclear

## üÜö Editorial Swiss vs. Classic Swiss Design

| Aspect | Classic Swiss | Editorial Swiss |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Primary Use** | Posters, identity | Articles, long-form content |
| **Typography** | Sans-serif (Helvetica) | Serif body + sans headings |
| **Body text size** | Functional (14-16px) | Generous (18-21px) |
| **Line height** | 1.4-1.5 | 1.7-1.8 (more readable) |
| **Layout** | Multi-column grids | Single-column preferred |
| **Priority** | Visual impact | Reading experience |
| **Hierarchy** | Through size/weight | Dramatic, clear (large h1) |
| **Content** | Brief, impactful | Long-form, structured |
| **Examples** | Posters, logos | Magazines, blogs, documentation |

**Relationship:** Editorial Swiss applies Swiss principles (clarity, hierarchy, grid) specifically to long-form content and reading experiences.

## üí° Tips for Authentic Editorial Swiss

**Study Neue Grafik:**
1958-1965 magazine is the gold standard for Editorial Swiss. Study their typographic treatment.

**Prioritize Readability:**
Every choice serves reading experience. Large text, generous line-height, optimal measure.

**Use Serif for Body:**
Serif typefaces (Charter, Georgia, Freight Text) feel more editorial and readable for long-form.

**Dramatic Hierarchy:**
h1 should be significantly larger than body (2.5-3x size). Don't be timid.

**Generous Line-Height:**
1.7-1.8 for body text. Long-form needs breathing room.

**Optimal Measure:**
65-75 characters per line. If wider, reduce max-width.

**Wide Margins:**
Frame content with generous side margins. Don't fill entire viewport.

**Vertical Rhythm:**
Consistent spacing (24-32px paragraphs, 64px before h2). Follow baseline grid.

**Pull Quotes:**
Highlight key ideas with large serif quotes (28-36px), centered or offset.

**Reference Medium.com:**
Contemporary example of Editorial Swiss for web. Study their typography and spacing.
